## Segment 1: Individual Hosts & Micro-Operators

### 1A — The Side-Hustle Host

**Archetype:** A working professional who manages 1–5 vacation rental properties as supplemental income alongside a full-time career.

**Representative Users:** Ben Carter (Teacher, 2 beach cottages, OBX), Ava Williams (Graphic designer, 1 property pre-launch), David Miller (Part-time host, 2 properties)

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Age Range** | 28–50 |
| **Properties** | 1–5 |
| **Guesty Tier** | Lite |
| **Tech Proficiency** | Intermediate |
| **Primary Motivation** | Supplemental income, financial independence |
| **Time Available** | Limited (evenings/weekends around day job) |

**Core Responsibilities:**
- Responding to guest inquiries and booking requests (primarily via OTAs)
- Managing booking calendars to prevent double-bookings
- Coordinating cleaning turnovers with a single trusted cleaner
- Sending check-in instructions and handling in-stay guest issues
- Occasional listing updates (photos, descriptions)
- Tracking expenses for tax purposes

**Guesty Features Used:**
- Multi-Calendar (consolidated view of bookings)
- Unified Inbox (managing messages from Airbnb/Vrbo in one place)
- Channel Manager (syncing Airbnb + Vrbo)
- Basic Automation Tools (confirmation messages, check-in instructions)
- Mobile App (responding on-the-go during work hours)

**Key Pain Points:**
- Time scarcity — must manage rentals around a demanding day job
- Fear of double-bookings or missed messages
- Price-sensitive about software subscriptions
- Limited budget for dynamic pricing tools or marketing
- Reliance on a single cleaner creates fragility
- Keeping up with local STR regulations and permits

**Decision-Making Style:**
- Prioritizes affordability and ease of use
- Reads host forums, Facebook groups, YouTube reviews
- Looks for free trials and low-cost entry plans
- Values "peace of mind" and time savings over feature depth

**What Success Looks Like:**
- 70–80% occupancy in peak season
- 4.5+ star average review scores
- Positive cash flow after all expenses
- Minimal daily time spent on rental management

- Growing to 25–50 properties within 12–24 months
- Hiring a full-time operations person

- 15%+ direct bookings
