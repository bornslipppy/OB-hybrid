# Product Brief: Calendar-Based Automations for Inbox v2

<!-- Content will be appended sequentially through collaborative workflow steps -->

## Core Vision

### The Problem We're Solving

**Primary Problem:**
Property managers operating at scale (50-200+ listings) are trapped in manual operational messaging workflows that don't scale. They spend an estimated **400+ hours weekly** across the customer base manually activating and deactivating recurring operational messages (trash day reminders, pool service notifications, lawn care alerts, weekend prep messages) because Guesty's automated message system cannot trigger based on calendar patterns like "every Thursday" or "weekends only."

**Current Workaround:**
- **Manual activate/deactivate cycles:** PMs log in every week to turn on "Trash Day Reminder" on Wednesday morning, then turn it off Thursday morning
- **Spreadsheet tracking:** Managing which properties need which operational messages on which days
- **Message duplication:** Creating 7 separate messages (one per day) when they need "weekday vs weekend" logic
- **Team communication overhead:** Coordinating who activated what, when

**Scale Breaking Point:**
- At **10 properties:** Manual workarounds are tedious but manageable
- At **50-70 properties:** Manual management becomes operationally untenable (can't remember all patterns)
- At **100+ properties:** Automation is survival — without it, operational messaging collapses

**Quantified Impact:**
- **80 accounts** (representing **$189,094 MRR**) have formally requested this capability
- **2,860+ support tickets annually** stem from automated message complexity (46% of all tickets)
- **40% of "Failed to Send" tickets** = customers misunderstanding condition logic while trying to work around missing calendar features
- Feature has been **"requested for over a year now, and growing in demand"** (strategic vision document)

**Who Experiences This Most Acutely:**
- **Primary:** Mid-Market segment (50-200 properties) — managing operational complexity without enterprise resources
- **Secondary:** SMB operators (21-50 properties) scaling into mid-market pain
- **Tertiary:** Enterprise (500+) — already expect this as table stakes (competitors have it)

**Strategic Context:**
This is not a "nice-to-have" — this is **the #1 most requested feature in the entire Communications domain** and a competitive gap that is actively handicapping customer growth and satisfaction.

---

### Our Solution

**Calendar-Based Automation Engine** — A flexible scheduling system that allows property managers to define when automated messages should trigger based on calendar patterns rather than just reservation events.

**Core Capabilities (Phase 1 - MVP):**

1. **Day-of-Week Triggers** (Primary use case)
   - Enable: "Send this message every Thursday at 8:00 AM"
   - Enable: "Send this message on weekdays only" or "weekends only"
   - Combine with existing reservation conditions (e.g., "Thursday before check-in")

2. **Specific Date/Date Range Triggers** (Secondary use case)
   - Enable: "Send during holiday weekends only" (Memorial Day, July 4th, etc.)
   - Enable: "Activate this message from June 1 - August 31" (seasonal operations)
   - Support recurring annual patterns

3. **Time-of-Day Scheduling** (Tertiary use case)
   - Enable: "Send at 8:00 AM local property time" (not guest timezone)
   - Support multiple send times for same message pattern

**Design Principles:**
- **Set it and forget it:** Configure once, runs automatically forever
- **Combine, don't replace:** Works WITH existing reservation-based triggers (not instead of)
- **Clear mental model:** "When should this send?" vs current "What conditions enable this?"
- **Reduce complexity:** Simplify the 40% of failed sends caused by condition misunderstanding

**Success Looks Like:**
- Mid-market PM with 70 properties sets up "Thursday trash reminders" **once** → saves 2 hours weekly
- Support tickets for "AM Failed to Send" drop by **40%** (condition confusion eliminated)
- Customers stop duplicating messages to hack day-of-week logic (cleaner workflow library)
- Guesty closes automation gap with Track and Streamline (competitive parity restored)

### What Makes This Different

**1. Competitive Catch-Up (Closing the Gap)**
- **Track:** ✅ Already has calendar-based conditions
- **Streamline:** ✅ Already has calendar-based conditions
- **Guesty:** ❌ Currently lagging (3★ vs 4★ in automations)
- **This feature:** Restores competitive parity and eliminates a known blocker for mid-market growth

**2. Operational vs. Transactional Focus**
- Most existing AM features focus on **guest journey messaging** (booking confirmation, check-in instructions, thank you notes)
- This feature unlocks **operational messaging** (recurring maintenance, weekly reminders, seasonal instructions)
- **Differentiation:** We're not just automating guest comms — we're automating property operations

**3. Cross-Segment Value (Not Just Enterprise)**
- Unlike "team tagging" or "call center queues" (enterprise-only requests)
- **This feature serves Small → SMB → Mid-Market → Enterprise** (universal pain point)
- Enables growth from 20 → 50 → 100+ properties without operational cliff

**4. Support Burden Relief (Internal Differentiator)**
- Addresses **46% of all support tickets** (AM complexity)
- Reduces **40% of "Failed to Send" escalations** (condition confusion)
- Creates clearer mental model for customers: "When?" instead of "What conditions enable?"
- **Internal win:** Frees support team to focus on actual product issues vs. knowledge gaps

**5. Foundation for Future Features**
- **Phase 2 enabler:** Seasonal campaigns, promotional windows, holiday-specific messaging
- **Strategic positioning:** "Guesty helps you automate not just guest communication, but property operations"
- **Monetization potential:** Premium tier differentiator (competitors charge for this)
